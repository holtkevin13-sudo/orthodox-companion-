Akathist to St. Seraphim of Sarov

The Akathist Hymn to St. Seraphim of Sarov — one of the most beloved saints of the Orthodox world, known for his joy, humility, and spiritual radiance.

← Back to Prayer Corner
Akathist Hymn
Prayed on January 2 (his glorification) and July 19 (his repose), and throughout the year by those who love him.
St. Seraphim of Sarov (1754–1833) is one of the most universally beloved saints of the Orthodox world. He is known for his joyful greeting to all who came to him — "My joy, Christ is Risen!" — for his radiant humility, for his great love for the Theotokos, and for his teaching that the goal of Christian life is the acquisition of the Holy Spirit. He spent years in the forest in silence and prayer, and then opened his doors to thousands of pilgrims. His akathist is prayed across all Orthodox jurisdictions.
Kontakion 1
O chosen wonderworker and great saint, O holy Father Seraphim, who didst shine forth in the Russian land as a bright star: we offer thee our song of praise. But as thou hast boldness before the Lord, deliver us from all troubles and tribulations that we may cry to thee:
Rejoice, O Seraphim, venerable Father and wonderworker of Sarov!
Oikos 1
An angel in the flesh wast thou shown to be on earth, O Father Seraphim. From thy youth thou didst love Christ above all things, and the Most Holy Theotokos didst thou choose as thy Guide. Therefore we cry to thee: Rejoice, thou who from thy childhood wast chosen by God; Rejoice, thou who wast guarded by the Queen of Heaven! Rejoice, thou who wast consumed with love for God; Rejoice, thou who didst set thy heart on heavenly things! Rejoice, flame of divine love; Rejoice, lamp of monastic virtue! Rejoice, thou who didst greet all with joy in the name of the Risen Christ; Rejoice, O Seraphim, venerable Father and wonderworker of Sarov!
Rejoice, O Seraphim, venerable Father and wonderworker of Sarov!
Kontakion 2
Seeing the light of divine grace shine upon thee, O blessed Father, the holy Elder Nazarius received thee into the monastery, and there thou didst labor night and day in prayer and fasting, crying to God:
Alleluia!
Oikos 2
Understanding that the highest calling is the acquisition of the Holy Spirit, thou didst give thyself entirely to prayer and repentance, spending a thousand days and nights in prayer upon a rock in the forest: Rejoice, thou whose prayer was ceaseless; Rejoice, thou who wast nourished by the Bread of Life! Rejoice, thou who didst tame the passions of the body; Rejoice, thou who didst illumine the darkness of sin! Rejoice, thou whose heart became a temple of the Holy Spirit; Rejoice, O Seraphim, venerable Father and wonderworker of Sarov!
Rejoice, O Seraphim, venerable Father and wonderworker of Sarov!
Kontakion 3 — 12
[The full Akathist to St. Seraphim continues through 13 kontakia and oikoi, recalling his life in the forest hermitage, his encounters with robbers whom he forgave, the appearance of the Theotokos to him, his years of reclusion, and the thousands he received and healed when he opened his cell. His greeting — "My joy, Christ is Risen!" — echoes through the hymn. The complete text is available in Orthodox prayer books.]
Kontakion 13
O most holy Father Seraphim, accept our humble prayer and intercede for us before the Lord. Thou who didst greet all with joy, make us worthy to share in the joy of Thy risen Lord, that with thee we may cry:
Alleluia!
Troparion of St. Seraphim
Thou didst love Christ from thy youth, O blessed one, and to Him alone thou didst yearn to cleave with burning desire. Keeping thyself pure in heart in the wilderness, thou didst find the Kingdom of Heaven, and having received the gift of healing, thou dost heal all who come to thee. Therefore we cry to thee: save us by thy prayers, O Seraphim, our venerable Father.
