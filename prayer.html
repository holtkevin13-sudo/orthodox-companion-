Prayer Corner

Enter the rhythm of Orthodox prayer — morning and evening, psalms and liturgical hymns, the Jesus Prayer, canons and akathists, and prayers for every occasion of life.

Orthodox prayer is not a collection of texts to recite — it is a rhythm of life, and its goal is communion with the living God in Christ Jesus. These prayers are not separate from the worship of the Church: they flow from the Divine Liturgy and return to it, sustaining the Christian life between one Eucharist and the next. It is the Holy Spirit who makes this prayer possible at all — "We do not know how to pray as we ought," St. Paul writes, "but the Spirit Himself intercedes for us" (Romans 8:26). The Church gives us morning and evening prayers, the Psalter, the Jesus Prayer, and the liturgical cycle as the form in which the Holy Spirit prays through us, gradually drawing us into union with Christ. Begin where you are, remain consistent, and let the prayers of the Church become your own.

"Let my prayer arise as incense before Thee, the lifting up of my hands as an evening sacrifice."
Psalm 140:2 (LXX) — chanted at every Orthodox Vespers
"The rule exists to serve prayer, not prayer to serve the rule. If you are rushed, say fewer prayers with full attention rather than many prayers hastily."
— St. Theophan the Recluse
