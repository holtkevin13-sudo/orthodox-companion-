Our Mission

Why This Site Exists

Orthodox Companion was built out of a simple conviction: the fullness of Orthodox Christianity should be accessible to anyone who seeks it, wherever they are in the journey.

"The Church has always been a hospital for the soul — open to all who come broken, and gentle enough to receive the curious as readily as the devout."

Too many people discover Orthodoxy through a podcast, a book, or a friend's invitation — and then face a gap. They cannot attend services easily. They do not know where to begin. The language of the liturgy, the logic of the calendar, the rhythm of the fasting life — it can feel opaque from the outside. This site exists to bridge that gap.

Orthodox Companion is not a substitute for the parish, the priest, or the community. It is a companion — a gentle guide to the threshold and, if God wills, through it. Whether you are a catechumen, a lifelong Orthodox Christian deepening your practice, or simply a curious outsider looking in, you are welcome here.

About the Companion AI

🤖 An Honest Note About the AI Chatbot

The Companion AI is powered by the Claude API (Anthropic) and is designed to answer questions about Orthodox Christianity drawing on patristic sources, canonical texts, liturgical tradition, and the theological consensus of the Orthodox Church. It is not a priest and should not be treated as one — but it can be a helpful, patient, and well-informed conversation partner.

The Companion AI is a premium feature. Guest visitors receive a limited number of free questions. Full, unlimited access is available to registered members through an affordable subscription.

Subscriptions directly fund the continued development of this site — the calendar infrastructure, the content, the hosting, and future features. There are no ads here. Your subscription is what makes Orthodox Companion sustainable and free of commercial influence.

We are transparent about the AI's nature and its limitations. It can be wrong. It should complement — never replace — the guidance of your priest, your bishop, and the living community of the Church.
