About Orthodox Companion

A free, independent resource for understanding and living the Orthodox Christian faith — grounded in the patristic tradition, built for inquirers, catechumens, and lifelong Orthodox Christians alike.

What Is Orthodox Companion?

The Orthodox faith is not hard to find online. Clear, trustworthy, patristically grounded guides to it are.

Orthodox Companion exists because the internet is full of Orthodox-adjacent content — some of it excellent, much of it contradictory, some of it quietly misleading. Finding your footing as an inquirer, a catechumen, or even a lifelong Orthodox Christian should not require a seminary education to navigate.

This site was built to faithfully reflect the voice of the Church — not a personality, not a jurisdiction, not an opinion — but the received Tradition: Holy Scripture as the Church reads it, the Seven Ecumenical Councils, the Church Fathers, and the living worship of the Orthodox Church.

It is built for those exploring the faith for the first time, for those preparing for reception, and for those who have walked this path for decades and want a reliable, well-ordered place to study, pray, and prepare.

“Where the Church is, there is the Spirit of God; and where the Spirit of God is, there is the Church and every kind of grace.”

St. Irenaeus of Lyons — Against Heresies, III.24.1

What This Site Provides

Orthodox Companion brings together the major resources of the Orthodox faith in one integrated system — connected to each other, grounded in the same Tradition:

  • The liturgical calendar with fasting guidance
  • The Twelve Great Feasts and the full Paschal cycle
  • The Church Fathers and the saints with theological and biographical context
  • Theological topics and the vocabulary of Orthodox theology
  • Holy Scripture as the Church receives it
  • Reading plans and the prayer traditions of the Orthodox faithful

These are not isolated articles. They are linked to one another — feasts connect to theology, theology to the Fathers, the Fathers to Scripture — because the Orthodox faith is not a collection of subjects. It is one living whole.

What This Site Is Not

Orthodox Companion is not affiliated with any jurisdiction, archdiocese, or parish. It holds no canonical authority.

It is not a substitute for a spiritual father, for the Eucharist, for Confession, or for the life of the Church.

Orthodoxy is not primarily a body of information to be acquired. It is a way of life — lived in community, in fasting and prayer, and above all at the Holy Table. This site exists to support that life and to point you toward it.

Sources and Standards

All content is drawn from Holy Scripture as received and interpreted by the Church; the Seven Ecumenical Councils; the Church Fathers — East and West, ancient and recent; and the liturgical texts of the Orthodox Church.

Where contemporary sources are used, they are chosen for fidelity to the Tradition, not for popularity or novelty. This site strives to reflect the consensus of Orthodox theology. Where genuine diversity exists within Orthodoxy, it is named as such. Where something is disputed, it is not presented as settled.

Finding a Parish

If you are inquiring about Orthodoxy — or want to understand it more deeply — the most important step you can take is to attend a Divine Liturgy.

No article and no guide can give you what the Liturgy gives you. The Orthodox faith is encountered, not downloaded. It lives in the worship of the Church, in the ancient chants, in the Body and Blood of Christ given to His people.

Use the Parish Finder to find an Orthodox parish near you.

“We have seen the true Light; we have received the heavenly Spirit; we have found the true faith, worshipping the undivided Trinity, who has saved us.”

Divine Liturgy of St. John Chrysostom

This is what Orthodox Companion is for — not to give you the faith, but to bring you to the place where it lives. Everything here points beyond itself: to the parish, to the priest, to the Holy Table, and to the living God who saves us.
