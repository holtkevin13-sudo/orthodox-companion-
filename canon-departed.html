Canon for the Departed

The Canon for the Departed — prayed on Soul Saturdays and at home commemorations for loved ones who have fallen asleep in the hope of the Resurrection.

← Back to Prayer Corner
Canon of Intercession for the Dead
Prayed on Soul Saturdays throughout the year and at home commemorations for loved ones who have reposed. Also prayed on the anniversary of a death.
The Orthodox Church prays for the departed not as a futile gesture but in the living faith that God's mercy is not bounded by death, and that our prayer has real meaning for those who have fallen asleep in Christ. Soul Saturdays are appointed throughout the liturgical year for the commemoration of all the faithful departed. This canon is prayed at home as a personal act of love and intercession for those who have gone before us.
Before the Canon
Blessed is our God, always, now and ever and unto the ages of ages. Amen. Glory to Thee, our God, glory to Thee. Remember, O Lord, the souls of Thy departed servants: [names of the departed].
Ode 1 — Irmos
He is my helper and protector, and has become my salvation. This is my God and I will glorify Him; my father's God and I will exalt Him; for gloriously has He been glorified.
Ode 1 — Troparia
Give rest, O Lord, to the souls of Thy servants who have fallen asleep, and account them worthy of the eternal habitations of the saints, O Good One. O God of spirits and of all flesh, Who hast trampled down death and overthrown the Devil, and given life to Thy world: give rest, O Lord, to the souls of Thy departed servants in a place of brightness, a place of refreshment, a place of repose, where all sickness, sighing, and sorrow have fled away. As a good and loving God, forgive every sin committed by the departed in thought, word, and deed, O Lover of mankind.
Ode 3 — Irmos
There is none holy as Thou, O Lord my God, Who hast exalted the horn of Thy faithful and established us on the rock of Thy confession.
Ode 3 — Troparia
O Lord, Who dost possess the power of life and death: grant rest to the souls of those who have departed from us, and account them worthy of the Kingdom of Heaven. Pardon every transgression committed in this life, O Christ, by those who have fallen asleep; for Thou alone art without sin, and Thou lovest mankind.
Sessional Hymn
Truly all is vanity and a shadow and a dream. For in a moment all the earthly splendors pass away. For as the wind scattereth the dust, and as the snow melteth, so is man dissolved. But Christ, being eternal, doth call upon the sleepers in the tombs: Come, all ye peoples, and arise from the dead.
Ode 4 — Troparia
Give rest, O Lord, to those who have departed from us and have hoped in Thee; for Thou art the Resurrection and the Life, and the Repose of Thy servants. Grant to those who have fallen asleep the inheritance of the Kingdom of Heaven, O Savior, since they were washed in the saving waters of Baptism and received the seal of the Holy Spirit.
Ode 5 — Troparia
O Immortal King, in Thy wisdom Thou hast fashioned all things. Receive the souls of those who have departed in the faith of the Holy Trinity, and make them to dwell in the light of Thy countenance. Remember all the Orthodox who have fallen asleep, O Lord, and grant them rest where the light of Thy countenance visits them.
Ode 6 — Kontakion for the Departed
With the saints give rest, O Christ, to the souls of Thy servants, where there is neither sickness, nor sorrow, nor suffering, but life everlasting. Thou only art immortal, Who hast created and fashioned man. For from earth were we made, and to earth shall we return as Thou hast commanded Who madest me, saying: Earth thou art and to earth shalt thou return. Whither all we mortals go, making our funeral lamentation the song: Alleluia, Alleluia, Alleluia.
Ode 7 — Troparia
O Lord, Who savest from corruption those who call upon Thee: grant rest to those who have completed their earthly course and now sleep in peace. Grant rest with the righteous, O Lord, to the souls of Thy servants who have fallen asleep, for Thou art good and lovest mankind.
Ode 8 — Troparia
Let all creation bless the Lord: and we bless and glorify Him for granting rest to the departed, in His mercy and love for mankind. Remember, O Lord, the souls of those who have fallen asleep in the hope of the Resurrection, and grant them rest in the land of the living.
Ode 9 — Irmos
It is truly right to bless thee, O Theotokos, ever blessed and most pure and Mother of our God. More honorable than the Cherubim, more glorious beyond compare than the Seraphim, who without corruption gavest birth to God the Word, thee do we magnify.
Ode 9 — Troparia
O most pure Theotokos, intercede for the souls of those who have fallen asleep, that they may find mercy at the dread Judgment of thy Son. All our departed, O Christ, who have died in faith and hope: grant them rest, forgiveness and salvation, for Thou art compassionate.
Prayer for the Departed — After the Canon
O God of spirits and of all flesh, Who hast trampled down death and overthrown the Devil, and given life to Thy world: Give rest, O Lord, to the souls of Thy departed servants [names] in a place of brightness, a place of refreshment, a place of repose, where all sickness, sighing, and sorrow have fled away. Pardon every transgression which they have committed, whether by word or deed or thought. For Thou art good and lovest mankind. There is no man who lives and does not sin; for Thou only art without sin, Thy righteousness is an everlasting righteousness, and Thy word is truth. For Thou art the Resurrection and the Life and the Repose of Thy departed servants, O Christ our God, and unto Thee we ascribe glory, together with Thy Father, Who is from everlasting, and Thine all-holy and good and life-creating Spirit, now and ever and unto the ages of ages. Amen.
