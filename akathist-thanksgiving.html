Akathist of Thanksgiving — Glory to God for All Things

The complete Akathist of Thanksgiving, "Glory to God for All Things," composed by Protopresbyter Gregory Petrov before his death in a Soviet labor camp.

← Back to Prayer Corner
Akathist Hymn
Prayed throughout the year as an act of thanksgiving. Particularly moving during times of suffering, loss, or trial.
This akathist was composed by Protopresbyter Gregory Petrov (1880–1940) shortly before his death in a Soviet labor camp. Written in conditions of unimaginable suffering, it is a sustained hymn of praise and thanksgiving for the beauty of God's creation and the grace of existence. It has become one of the most beloved texts in the modern Orthodox world, prayed across all traditions as a witness that gratitude is possible in all things.
Kontakion 1
Everlasting King, Thy will for our salvation is full of mercy. After the Fall Thou didst not turn away from us, but by the blood of Thine Only-Begotten Son Thou didst reconcile heaven and earth. Help us who have turned from sin to Thee, fill our hearts with joy that we may sing:
Glory to God for all things!
Oikos 1
I was born a weak, defenseless child, but Thine angel spread his wings over my cradle to defend me. From birth until now Thy love has illumined my path, and has wondrously guided me towards the light of eternity; from birth until now the generous gifts of Thy Providence have been marvelously showered upon me. I give Thee thanks, with all who have come to know Thee, who call upon Thy name.
Glory to God for all things!
Oikos 2
Lord, how lovely it is to be Thy guest. Breeze full of scents; mountains reaching to the skies; waters like boundless mirrors, reflecting the sun's golden rays and the scudding clouds. All nature hums mysteriously, breathing the depths of tenderness. Birds and beasts of the forest bear the imprint of Thy love. Blessed art thou, mother earth, in thy fleeting loveliness, which wakens our longing for happiness that will last for ever, where amid beauty that grows not old, the cry will sound: Alleluia!
Glory to God for all things!
Oikos 3
Thou hast brought me into life as into an enchanted paradise. We have seen the sky, deep as the fathomless blue water, into its outer reaches, and a sunset hath drenched the sky in scarlet, gold, azure and green; we have seen transparent clouds drift slowly by, and we have heard the singing of the birds; we have enjoyed sweet-smelling flowers and delicious fruits. Our consolation is to live in Thy world, so beautiful, so full of Thee.
Glory to God for all things!
Oikos 4
How gracious is Thy house! I know that the Spirit of God has here made His dwelling, and that Thou art present in this place through the outpouring of Thy grace. Here is the portal of Thy Kingdom. And from the divine Liturgy as if from a furnace of love, Thy grace goes out to us with all its fire, and we are carried by it as on a stream of love into the luminous world where dwell the perfected spirits of Thy saints. Here Alleluia is cried ceaselessly.
Glory to God for all things!
Oikos 5
There is no death, O Lord, for Thy servants, when they pass from earth to heaven; but rather a transition from the more sorrowful to the more joyful, from the temporal to the eternal. And how glorious is the moment of that last breath, when, leaving behind the mortal body, the spirit takes wing into the realms of Thy blessed eternity!
Glory to God for all things!
Oikos 6
Thou Who hast shown us the light of Thy knowledge, and didst gather us from the darkness of ignorance — glory to Thee! Thou Who hast made us to love, and to gather together in harmony — glory to Thee! Thou Who hast helped us in the hour of trial and suffering — glory to Thee! Thou Who art the Giver of understanding and wisdom — glory to Thee! Thou Who dost gladden our hearts with the voices of Thy servants — glory to Thee for all things!
Glory to God for all things!
Oikos 7 — The Praise of Creation
Blessed art Thou for the lightning and thunder, O Lord, which proclaim Thy greatness. Blessed art Thou for the mountains and their majestic height. Blessed art Thou for the sea's expanse with its rumbling waves. Blessed art Thou for the rivers and their flowing murmur. Blessed art Thou for the springs hidden in the earth. Blessed art Thou for the morning dew and the evening mist. Blessed art Thou for the smells of the earth after the rain. Blessed art Thou for the life given to us, this brief taste of eternity.
Glory to God for all things!
Kontakion 13 — Closing
O all-merciful God, hear our prayer and preserve us in Thy grace, in the knowledge of Thy truth, and in the spirit of thanksgiving, that we may cry to Thee always, for all things and in all things:
Glory to God for all things!
Note
The complete Akathist of Thanksgiving consists of 13 oikoi and 13 kontakia. The text above includes the foundational oikoi and the spirit of the complete hymn. The full text is available in Orthodox prayer books and at Ancient Faith Publishing.
