Canon of Repentance to Our Lord Jesus Christ

The Canon of Repentance to Our Lord Jesus Christ — the first of the three Pre-Communion canons, and a powerful penitential prayer for any time.

← Back to Prayer Corner
Penitential Canon
Read the evening before Holy Communion as the first of the three Pre-Communion canons. Also prayed independently as a penitential canon at any time.
The Canon of Repentance is a nine-ode canon addressed directly to Christ, deeply penitential in character and rich with Christological content. It is the first of the three canons traditionally read the evening before Holy Communion, alongside the Canon to the Theotokos and the Canon to the Guardian Angel. It is also widely prayed independently as a private act of repentance.
Prayer Before Reading the Canon
O Lord Jesus Christ, Son of God, for the sake of the prayers of Thy most pure Mother, of our holy and God-bearing fathers and all the saints, have mercy on us. Amen. Glory to Thee, our God, glory to Thee.
Ode 1 — Irmos
He is my helper and protector, and has become my salvation. This is my God and I will glorify Him; my father's God and I will exalt Him; for gloriously has He been glorified.
Ode 1 — Troparia
O Christ my Savior, have mercy on me, a sinner, and deliver me from all evil, and heal my soul, for I am sick and weak in my sin. I have stained my mind with earthly impurity and as a slave I have served the devil. But do Thou, O Lord, deliver me from this bondage and cleanse me. Woe is me, a sinner! I have spent my life in negligence and have come to the end of it without repentance. Do not, O Lord, condemn me. Have mercy on me, O God, have mercy on me, O Lord. I am not worthy to lift mine eyes to heaven, but look upon me and save me, for Thou art all-powerful.
Ode 3 — Irmos
There is none holy as Thou, O Lord my God, Who hast exalted the horn of Thy faithful and established us on the rock of Thy confession.
Ode 3 — Troparia
Heal my soul, O Lord, for I have sinned against Thee. Save me for Thy mercy's sake, for there is none holy beside Thee, O Lord. To Thee do I cry, O Lord; hear me and have mercy. I am sick in soul; heal me, for with Thee is the fountain of healing. I know, O Lord, that I am not worthy of Thy love. But be Thou Thyself my helper, and rescue me.
Kontakion
My soul, my soul, arise! Why art thou sleeping? The end is drawing near and thou wilt be confounded. Awake, then, and be watchful, that Christ thy God may spare thee, Who is everywhere present and filleth all things.
Ode 4 — Irmos
Christ is my power, my God and my Lord, the holy Church divinely singeth, crying with a pure mind, keeping festival in the Lord.
Ode 4 — Troparia
Grant me tears, O Christ my God, as once Thou didst grant them to the sinful woman, and count me worthy to anoint Thy feet which delivered me from the path of error and guided me in the way of salvation. What shall I call Thee? The tax collector's words cannot express my repentance. But be Thou Thyself my helper, and accept my prayer.
Ode 5 — Irmos
By Thy divine light illumine, O Good One, the souls of those who with love rise early to seek Thee, I pray Thee; that they may know Thee, O Word of God, as the true God Who recalleth us from the darkness of sin.
Ode 5 — Troparia
My whole life have I passed in darkness and ignorance. Illumine me, O Christ, with the light of Thy countenance. I dare not lift mine eyes to heaven. But look upon me with mercy, O Master, and have compassion on the work of Thy hands.
Ode 6 — Irmos
I pour out my prayer to the Lord, and to Him do I declare my grief; for my soul is filled with sorrow and my life hath drawn nigh unto hell, and I pray like Jonah: Lead me up from corruption, O God.
Ode 6 — Troparia
As Thou didst hear Jonah from the belly of the whale, O Christ, hear also me, a sinner, as I cry to Thee from the depths of my iniquity. O Christ, Son of God, Thou Who alone art sinless, have mercy on me, who am the greatest of sinners; for with Thee is the fountain of forgiveness.
Ode 7 — Irmos
The children of the Hebrews in the furnace bravely trod upon the flame of fire and changed the fire into dew, as they cried: Blessed art Thou, O Lord God, unto the ages.
Ode 7 — Troparia
I have been as one of the irrational beasts, O Lord, having given myself over to the passions. But receive me as the prodigal son, for I return to Thee. I have fallen greatly, O Lord. Help me to rise again and turn to Thee, that I may cry: Blessed art Thou, O Lord God of our Fathers.
Ode 8 — Irmos
The King of Heaven, Whom the angelic hosts glorify, praise and exalt above all unto the ages.
Ode 8 — Troparia
I have sinned, O Lord, I have sinned against Thee; be merciful to me. There is no sin of mine which Thou canst not forgive, O Lover of mankind. Accept me as Thou didst accept the thief, the publican, the harlot and the prodigal, and save me from the torment that awaiteth sinners.
Ode 9 — Irmos
It is truly right to bless thee, O Theotokos, who art most blessed, most pure, and Mother of our God. More honourable than the Cherubim and incomparably more glorious than the Seraphim, thou who without corruption gavest birth to God the Word, thee who art truly the Mother of God do we magnify.
Ode 9 — Troparia
O most merciful Lord, Who dost call all sinners to repentance: do not turn away from me, for I come to Thee and fall down before Thee, O Lord. O most pure Theotokos, who dost intercede for all the world: intercede also for me, a sinner, that I may find mercy at the awesome Judgment of thy Son. O Lord, Who wast incarnate for us sinners and endured the Cross: remember me in Thy Kingdom, as Thou didst remember the thief who cried to Thee from the Cross.
Prayer After the Canon
O Lord Jesus Christ my God, forgive me all my sins according to Thy great mercy, for the sake of the prayers of Thy most pure Mother, of all Thy saints, and of those who have been well-pleasing to Thee from the beginning of the world. Amen.
