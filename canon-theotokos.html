Supplication Canon to the Theotokos — Paraklesis

The Small Supplication Canon (Paraklesis) to the Most Holy Theotokos — prayed during the Dormition Fast and in times of sorrow, illness, or urgent need.

← Back to Prayer Corner
Supplication Canon — Paraklesis
Prayed throughout the Dormition Fast (August 1–14) and at any time of sorrow, illness, or urgent need. The second of the three Pre-Communion canons.
The Paraklesis (from the Greek meaning "supplication" or "comfort") is a canon of intercession to the Most Holy Theotokos. The Small Paraklesis is attributed to the Emperor Theodore II Laskaris (13th century) and alternates with the Great Paraklesis during the Dormition Fast. It is one of the most beautiful and consoling texts in the Orthodox tradition — a sustained cry to the Mother of God from the depths of human sorrow, illness, and helplessness.
Opening Prayers
Blessed is our God, always, now and ever and unto the ages of ages. Amen. Glory to Thee, our God, glory to Thee. Heavenly King, Comforter, Spirit of Truth, Who art everywhere present and fillest all things, Treasury of good things and Giver of life: come and dwell in us and cleanse us from every stain, and save our souls, O Good One.
Opening Canon Verse
Many are the multitudes of my sins; to thee, O Mother of God, do I run, seeking consolation. Visit my ailing soul and intercede with thy Son and our God, that He may grant me forgiveness for the evil deeds I have done, O only blessed one.
Ode 1 — Irmos
Let us, the faithful, take refuge in the protection of the Mother of God and let us all sing praises to her, rejoicing, as we call out from the depths of our souls: Hail, O Lady, our help, our joy and our protection, and the salvation of our souls.
Ode 1 — Troparia
O Theotokos, despise not the supplication of thy servants; but as thou art good, set us free from every calamity and peril, for we have no other help but thee, and we call upon thee in our afflictions. Go not past our supplication, O Holy Virgin, but receive it and bring it before the Lord thy God, the Lover of mankind, O blessed one, that He may grant forgiveness and salvation to those who honor thee.
Ode 3 — Irmos
O Lady, grant me refuge under the shelter of thy mercy, and guide me toward the divine light of thy Son, O all-hymned one.
Sessional Hymn
Intercede, O Theotokos, with the God Who is merciful and loveth mankind, that He may turn away His wrath from us and grant healing to our souls. We have thee as our fervent intercessor before God Who loveth mankind.
Ode 4 — Irmos
I heard, O Lord, the report of Thy dispensation, and I glorified Thee, O only Lover of mankind.
Ode 4 — Troparia
O Virgin, heal the wounds of my soul, putting an end to the assaults of the passions and the onslaughts of sins, by granting me compunction. Thou art a haven and protection, a wall of refuge and a joyful rejoicing, O Lady, to those who flee to thee in faith; thus do we all glorify thee.
Ode 5 — Irmos
O Lady, illumine us with the light of thy Son's commandments, and guide our steps in the paths of repentance, we pray thee.
Ode 6 — Irmos
My prayer will I pour out unto the Lord, and to Him will I announce my grief; for my soul hath been filled with sorrow, and my life hath drawn near to hell. Like Jonah will I pray: Lead me up from corruption, O God.
Ode 6 — Troparia
We are encompassed by many temptations, and we flee to thee for shelter, O pure Virgin Theotokos: turn away all dangers and afflictions from us. Sick am I in body, and I come to thee, the fount of healing. O Mother of God, bring healing to my body, and salvation to my soul.
Kontakion
O Theotokos, I have no other help, nor do I have any other hope apart from thee, O pure one. For I place my hope in thee. Grant me thy protection, that I despair not.
Ikos
Implore thee do I, O pure one, the cause of great joy and redemption of Adam and the weeping of Eve, the only consolation of those who are in tribulation and sorrow. Come to our aid now, O Mother of God, and make us worthy to obtain the gift of healing, and deliver all of us from all calamity and grief.
Ode 7 — Irmos
The three children in Babylon feared not the flame of the furnace, but cast into the midst of the fire, refreshed with dew, they sang: Blessed art Thou, O Lord God of our Fathers.
Ode 8 — Irmos
The army of heaven doth bless Thee, O Lord, as we all praise, bless, and worship Thee unto all the ages.
Ode 8 — Troparia
O Mother of God, thou art the vine that bore the true Grape, Christ our God, Who gave unto us the cup of salvation. Wherefore, with joy do we hymn thee unto all the ages. We pray thee, O most pure one, to intercede unceasingly on behalf of thine unworthy servants, that we may be delivered from every necessity and grief.
Ode 9 — Irmos
It is truly right to bless thee, the Theotokos, the ever-blessed and most pure, and the Mother of our God. More honorable than the Cherubim, and more glorious beyond compare than the Seraphim, thou who without corruption gavest birth to God the Word, we magnify thee.
Ode 9 — Troparia
O thou who art the joy of all who are in sorrow, the protectress of the afflicted, the nourisher of the poor, and the consolation of all, look upon us and have mercy on us. O Theotokos, thou art my strength, my protection and my refuge, the wall of my salvation and my joy. Cleanse me from every stain and save me.
Prayer After the Paraklesis
O most holy Lady Theotokos, accept this our humble supplication and present it to thy Son and our God, that through thy mediation He may save and preserve us. Intercede, O Lady, for those who are sick and in sorrow, grant consolation to those who are troubled, deliver the captives, preserve the children, care for the elderly, and bring all people to repentance. Amen.
