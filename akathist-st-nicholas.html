Akathist to St. Nicholas of Myra

The Akathist Hymn to St. Nicholas, Archbishop of Myra and Wonderworker — the most beloved saint akathist in the Orthodox world.

Akathist Hymn
Prayed especially on Thursdays (the weekday dedicated to St. Nicholas), on December 6 (his feast day), and on May 9 (the Translation of his relics).
St. Nicholas, Archbishop of Myra in Lycia (ca. 270–343), is the most universally beloved saint in the Orthodox world — venerated from Russia to Greece to the Arab world to Serbia. He is the great wonderworker, the defender of the oppressed, the protector of travelers and sailors, and the intercessor for those in desperate need. His akathist is among the most widely prayed of all saint akathists.
Kontakion 1
O chosen wonderworker and excellent minister of Christ, who pourest out for all the world an inexhaustible flood of miracles, I praise thee with love, O Nicholas. As thou hast boldness before the Lord, from all dangers free me, that I may cry to thee:
Rejoice, O Nicholas, great wonderworker!
Oikos 1
An angel in flesh wast thou revealed to be, O Nicholas, since thou didst live an angelic life on earth. For from thy very birth thou wast revealed to be holy, and thou didst fulfill in all things the will of God on earth. Wherefore, we call thee blessed: Rejoice, thou who before thy birth wast sanctified by God; Rejoice, thou who wast purified at thy very birth by grace! Rejoice, thou who even in infancy didst show forth virtue; Rejoice, O great wonderworker! Rejoice, thou who from thy youth wast a vessel of the Holy Spirit; Rejoice, thou who wast adorned with the grace of priesthood! Rejoice, thou who art an unashamed intercessor before God; Rejoice, thou who art a helper most ready for all who call upon thee!
Rejoice, O Nicholas, great wonderworker!
Kontakion 2
Seeing thee in a vision, O holy Father Nicholas, the servants of the Emperor Constantine saw thee as a mighty helper in their distress, and calling upon thee, they were released from unjust condemnation. Wherefore, giving thanks to God who glorifies His saints, they cried:
Alleluia!
Oikos 2
Understanding divine wisdom, blessed Nicholas, thou didst preserve the doctrine of the Trinity when the heresy of Arius threatened to divide the Church. Thou wast not silent before lies, but didst confess with boldness the true faith. Wherefore, we cry to thee: Rejoice, pillar and confirmation of Orthodoxy; Rejoice, zealous defender of the Holy Trinity! Rejoice, thou who didst strike Arius down in the sight of all; Rejoice, thou who didst protect the flock from the wolf! Rejoice, thou who art a swift helper to all who call upon thee; Rejoice, thou who art the consolation of all who are in distress!
Rejoice, O Nicholas, great wonderworker!
Kontakion 3 — 12
[The complete Akathist to St. Nicholas continues with Kontakia 3 through 12, each followed by an oikos of twelve salutations. These recall his intervention to save three unjustly condemned men, his provision for the three poor maidens, his calming of storms at sea, his many healings, and his unceasing intercession before God. The full text is found in Orthodox prayer books.]
Kontakion 13
O most holy Father Nicholas, accept this our offering of praise, and by thine intercession before God deliver us from all evil, from sudden and unexpected death, and from eternal punishment, that with thee we may cry to God:
Alleluia!
Troparion of St. Nicholas
The truth of things hath revealed thee to thy flock as a rule of faith, an icon of meekness, and a teacher of temperance; therefore thou hast achieved the heights by humility, riches by poverty. O Father Nicholas, intercede with Christ God that our souls may be saved.
Kontakion of St. Nicholas
In Myra thou wast a priest most holy, O Nicholas; for fulfilling the Gospel of Christ, thou didst lay down thy soul for thy people, and didst save the innocent from death. Therefore thou art blessed as one learned in the mysteries of God's grace.

Holy Father Nicholas, pray to God for us.
